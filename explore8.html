more about White Monastry

The White Monastery, known locally as Seto Gumba, stands as a serene haven on the outskirts of Kathmandu Valley, Nepal. Perched atop a hill in Ramkot, this Buddhist monastery captivates visitors with its striking white architecture contrasting beautifully against the verdant surroundings. Founded under the guidance of the esteemed Buddhist monk Thrangu Rinpoche, the monastery serves not only as a spiritual retreat but also as a cultural landmark offering profound insights into Tibetan Buddhist traditions. Visitors to the White Monastery are greeted with a tranquil environment conducive to contemplation and meditation. Its prayer halls are adorned with vibrant murals depicting Buddhist scriptures and teachings, providing a glimpse into Nepal's rich artistic heritage. The monastery's hilltop location offers sweeping panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley, making it an ideal spot to witness stunning sunsets that paint the sky with hues of orange and gold. Beyond its architectural beauty and natural vistas, the White Monastery invites visitors to participate in Buddhist rituals, teachings, and meditation practices. This cultural immersion allows guests to deepen their understanding of Tibetan Buddhist philosophy and spirituality while enjoying a peaceful retreat away from the city's hustle and bustle. Whether seeking spiritual solace, cultural exploration, or simply a moment of tranquility amidst natural beauty, the White Monastery offers a serene and enriching experience that leaves a lasting impression on all who visit.


How to get there

To reach the White Monastery (Seto Gumba) in Ramkot, Kathmandu, several transportation options are available: One of the most convenient ways is to take a taxi or arrange a private car from Kathmandu city center. Taxis are readily available and can take you directly to the entrance of the monastery. Ensure to specify Seto Gumba in Ramkot to the driver. For those preferring public transportation, you can take a public bus from various points in Kathmandu that list Ramkot as a destination. Buses are a budget-friendly option, although you may need to walk a short distance uphill from the bus stop to reach the monastery. If you're staying in nearby areas like Balaju or Tokha and enjoy walking, hiking to the monastery is also possible. The hike offers scenic views along the way and allows you to soak in the natural beauty of the surroundings. Alternatively, renting a motorbike or bicycle provides flexibility to explore at your own pace. Many rental shops in Kathmandu offer these options, making it convenient to navigate the city and reach destinations like the White Monastery. Upon arrival at the White Monastery, visitors can enjoy its serene environment, explore its prayer halls adorned with vibrant murals, and take in panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley. Whether arriving by taxi, bus, on foot, or by bike, the White Monastery offers a tranquil retreat and cultural experience that enriches any visit to Kathmandu.

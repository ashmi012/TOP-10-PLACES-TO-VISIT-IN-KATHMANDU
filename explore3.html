more about Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square is rich with cultural and historical attractions. Some of the major highlights include:

1. Hanuman Dhoka Palace: This palace complex served as the royal residence of Nepalese kings and is adorned with intricately carved wooden windows and doors.
2. Kumari Ghar: The residence of the Living Goddess Kumari, recognizable by its ornate traditional architecture and the Kumari's occasional appearances at the window.
3. Temples: Numerous temples dot the square, including Taleju Temple, Jagannath Temple, and Kal Bhairav Temple, each with its own unique architectural style and religious significance.
4. Statues and Sculptures:Throughout the square, visitors can find ancient stone sculptures and statues depicting various Hindu deities and mythical figures.
5. Museums: The square also houses museums like the Hanuman Dhoka Museum, offering insights into Nepal's royal history, art, and culture.
6. Cultural Performances: During festivals and special occasions, Kathmandu Durbar Square comes alive with cultural performances, rituals, and religious ceremonies that provide a deeper understanding of Nepalese traditions.
7. Architecture and Artwork: The square's architecture, characterized by pagoda-style roofs, intricately carved wooden structures, and stone artwork, showcases Nepal's rich artistic heritage and craftsmanship.
Visiting Kathmandu Durbar Square offers a fascinating journey through Nepal's royal past and cultural traditions, making it a cherished destination for history buffs, art enthusiasts, and spiritual seekers alike.


How to get there

To reach Swayambhunath Stupa, you can take a taxi or a local bus from various points in Kathmandu. Taxis are readily available and provide a convenient way to reach the hill where the stupa is located. If you prefer public transportation, several bus routes pass near the stupa, making it accessible from different parts of the city. From the nearest major areas like Thamel or Durbar Marg, it's a short drive or a moderate walk uphill to reach the entrance of Swayambhunath. Once there, a climb up a series of steps leads you to the main stupa complex, offering scenic views along the way.

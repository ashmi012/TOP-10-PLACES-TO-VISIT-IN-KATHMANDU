more about Seto Machhindranath temple

Seto Machhendranath Temple in Kathmandu is surrounded by several major attractions that make it a must-visit destination. The temple itself is a marvel of Newari architecture, with intricate wood carvings and a stunning pagoda-style roof. Nearby, visitors can explore the bustling Asan Bazaar, one of Kathmandu's oldest markets, known for its vibrant atmosphere and diverse range of goods, from spices and fresh produce to traditional handicrafts. Just a short walk away is the historic Kathmandu Durbar Square, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring an array of ancient palaces, courtyards, and temples, including the famous Hanuman Dhoka and the residence of the Living Goddess, Kumari. Additionally, the temple is close to the picturesque Garden of Dreams, a neo-classical garden perfect for relaxation amidst lush greenery and beautifully designed pavilions. Together, these attractions offer a rich blend of cultural, historical, and spiritual experiences, making the area around Seto Machhendranath Temple a vibrant and enriching destination for visitors.


How to get there

To reach Seto Machhendranath Temple in the heart of Kathmandu, you have several convenient options. If you're starting from central Kathmandu or nearby areas, taking a taxi or a ride-sharing service like Uber is the most straightforward and hassle-free way. Simply inform the driver that you want to go to Jana Bahal, also known as Machhindra Bahal, and they will drop you close to the temple entrance. For those preferring a more budget-friendly option, local public buses and microbuses run regularly through the city and have stops near Asan Bazaar, a short walk from the temple. Walking is also an enjoyable option if you're already exploring nearby attractions like Kathmandu Durbar Square or Thamel, as the temple is centrally located and easily accessible on foot. Regardless of your mode of transport, navigating to Seto Machhendranath Temple is relatively easy, allowing you to seamlessly integrate this cultural and spiritual site into your Kathmandu itinerary.

more about Swoyambhunath Stupa

Swayambhunath Stupa, also known as the Monkey Temple, is a captivating cultural and spiritual landmark in Kathmandu. Perched atop a hill, its iconic white dome topped with a golden spire is adorned with Buddha's eyes, symbolizing wisdom and compassion. Surrounding the stupa are numerous shrines, statues, and prayer wheels, creating a serene atmosphere for both pilgrims and visitors. The panoramic views from the hill offer a breathtaking vista of Kathmandu Valley and the majestic Himalayas, making Swayambhunath a significant destination for spiritual reflection and cultural exploration in Nepal.


How to get there

To reach Swayambhunath Stupa, you can take a taxi or a local bus from various points in Kathmandu. Taxis are readily available and provide a convenient way to reach the hill where the stupa is located. If you prefer public transportation, several bus routes pass near the stupa, making it accessible from different parts of the city. From the nearest major areas like Thamel or Durbar Marg, it's a short drive or a moderate walk uphill to reach the entrance of Swayambhunath. Once there, a climb up a series of steps leads you to the main stupa complex, offering scenic views along the way.

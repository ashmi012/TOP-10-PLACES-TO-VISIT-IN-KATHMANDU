more about Narayanhiti Royal Palace

Narayanhiti Royal Palace Museum in Kathmandu beckons visitors with a rich array of historical and cultural treasures. This former royal residence, once the center of Nepal's monarchy, showcases opulent living quarters, ceremonial halls, and intricately adorned rooms that highlight the grandeur and traditions of Nepalese royalty. From the Throne Room and Durbar Hall, where state functions were held, to the meticulously curated displays of royal artifacts and personal belongings, every corner of the palace offers a glimpse into Nepal's royal heritage. The palace grounds, with their serene gardens and architectural elegance, provide a peaceful backdrop for exploring centuries of history and cultural evolution. Narayanhiti Royal Palace Museum stands as a testament to Nepal's rich cultural legacy, inviting visitors to delve into its royal past and experience the grandeur of a bygone era firsthand.


How to get there

To get to Narayanhiti Royal Palace Museum in Kathmandu, there are several convenient transportation options available. Visitors can opt to take a taxi or use ride-sharing services like Uber, which provide direct access to the museum's entrance from various points within the city. Public buses also offer a budget-friendly way to reach the museum, with several routes passing through central Kathmandu. If you prefer a more leisurely approach, walking to the museum is viable, especially if you're staying in nearby areas such as Thamel or Durbar Marg. This allows you to soak in the bustling streets and local atmosphere on your way to this historic site. Additionally, renting a bicycle offers a flexible and eco-friendly means of transportation, providing the opportunity to explore Kathmandu's vibrant surroundings before arriving at Narayanhiti Royal Palace Museum. Upon arrival, visitors can enjoy exploring the palace grounds and experiencing Nepal's royal history and cultural heritage firsthand.

more about Singha Durbar

Singha Durbar, a majestic and historic governmental complex in Kathmandu, boasts several major attractions that make it a landmark of political and architectural significance. The grand palace itself, built in the early 20th century by Prime Minister Chandra Shumsher Jang Bahadur Rana, features a stunning blend of neoclassical European and traditional Nepalese architectural styles, with its impressive columns, ornate facades, and expansive gardens. Inside, visitors can explore beautifully decorated rooms and halls that once served as the administrative and residential spaces for the Rana prime ministers. The complex also houses several key government offices, including the Prime Minister's Office and various ministries, highlighting its ongoing role in Nepal's political landscape. Additionally, the palace grounds offer a glimpse into the country's history, with its sprawling lawns and historical significance making it a must-visit destination for those interested in Nepal's architectural and political heritage. Despite damage from the 2015 earthquake, restoration efforts continue to preserve Singha Durbar's grandeur and importance, ensuring it remains a central hub of governmental activity and a symbol of Nepal's rich history.


How to get there

To reach Singha Durbar in Kathmandu, there are several convenient options. Taking a taxi or using ride-sharing services like Uber is the easiest and most direct way, with drivers readily available throughout the city who are familiar with this prominent landmark. Alternatively, Kathmandu's extensive network of public buses and microbuses offers a budget-friendly option, with routes heading towards the city center and stopping near Singha Durbar. It's helpful to ask locals or check with your accommodation for specific bus routes and stops. For those exploring nearby areas on foot, such as Thamel or Durbar Marg, Singha Durbar is within walking distance, making it easily accessible by a pleasant stroll through the bustling streets of Kathmandu.
